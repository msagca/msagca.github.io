Hello, I'm Mustafa, a game developer from Ankara, Turkey. I hold a bachelor's degree in electrical and electronics engineering from [METU](https://www.metu.edu.tr/).

I began my career as a [digital design engineer](https://www.glassdoor.com/Career/digital-design-engineer-career_KO0,23.htm) at a company that developed digital front-end hardware and firmware for [5G](https://www.qualcomm.com/5g/what-is-5g) base stations. In this role, I led a team of engineers in the design, implementation, and verification of various networking and signal processing components, such as custom packet processors built on top of the UDP/IP stack. I worked with [FPGA](https://www.xilinx.com/products/silicon-devices/fpga/what-is-an-fpga.html)s and [SoC](https://www.xilinx.com/products/silicon-devices/soc.html)s, utilizing tools such as [Vivado](https://www.xilinx.com/products/design-tools/vivado.html) and [yosys](https://github.com/YosysHQ/yosys), and mainly coded in **Verilog**. I wrote **Python** and **Shell** scripts to automate different tasks and built **Embedded Linux** systems with [PetaLinux](https://www.xilinx.com/products/design-tools/embedded-software/petalinux-sdk.html) to provide a management interface for our end-users. Additionally, I managed the project source code and **CI/CD** workflows on the company's [GitLab](https://about.gitlab.com/) server. My team utilized [Scrum](https://www.scrum.org/learning-series/what-is-scrum), and we greatly benefited from it.

After spending four years in the telecommunications industry, I realized my longing to incorporate more visual elements and art into my work. However, continuing within the same profession would not allow me to pursue this aspiration. Consequently, I made the decision to shift my career towards the gaming industry. I opted for gaming due to its significant role in my life from an early age, coupled with the remarkable progress I've witnessed in game development over the years. Like many others, I began my journey with [Unity](https://unity.com/), which is a powerful tool for creating impressive interactive experiences with support for many platforms and has a vast community of developers who are willing to share their work and experience with others. Unity also provides an amazing platform called [Learn](https://learn.unity.com/), which allowed me to follow different pathways to learn about various aspects of game development by completing tasks and putting my skills into practice. I have completed [Unity Essentials](https://learn.unity.com/pathway/unity-essentials), [Junior Programmer](https://learn.unity.com/pathway/junior-programmer), and [Creative Core](https://learn.unity.com/pathway/creative-core) pathways, and gained a great deal of knowledge about game development with Unity.

Currently, I am working on several small projects of my own while continuing to learn about new technologies, such as Unity's [DOTS](https://unity.com/dots), in order to incorporate them into my games and make them more appealing to players. I am also experimenting with other game engines and various AI tools that have the potential to enhance my productivity. I will be documenting my progress on this website and have plans to publish some of my games on platforms like [itch.io](https://itch.io/).
