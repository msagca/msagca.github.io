Hello, I'm Mustafa, a game developer from Ankara, Turkey. I hold a bachelor's degree in electrical and electronics engineering from [METU](https://www.metu.edu.tr/).

I began my career as a [digital design engineer](https://www.glassdoor.com/Career/digital-design-engineer-career_KO0,23.htm) at a company that developed digital front-end hardware, firmware, and software for [5G](https://www.qualcomm.com/5g/what-is-5g) base stations. In this role, I led a team of engineers in the design, implementation, and verification of various networking and signal processing components, such as custom packet processors built on top of the UDP/IP stack. I worked with [FPGA](https://www.xilinx.com/products/silicon-devices/fpga/what-is-an-fpga.html)s and [SoC](https://www.xilinx.com/products/silicon-devices/soc.html)s, utilizing tools such as [Vivado](https://www.xilinx.com/products/design-tools/vivado.html) and [yosys](https://github.com/YosysHQ/yosys), and mainly coded in **Verilog**. I wrote **Python** and **Shell** scripts to automate different tasks and built **Embedded Linux** systems with [PetaLinux](https://www.xilinx.com/products/design-tools/embedded-software/petalinux-sdk.html) to provide a management interface for our end-users. Additionally, I managed the project source code and **CI/CD** workflows on the company's [GitLab](https://about.gitlab.com/) server. My team utilized [Scrum](https://www.scrum.org/learning-series/what-is-scrum), and we greatly benefited from its framework.

After successfully demonstrating an end-to-end 5G solution, which we had developed with our partners, at various events and receiving positive feedback, I realized that I wanted to incorporate more visual elements and art into my work. However, it wasn't possible if I continued in the same profession, so I decided to pursue a career in the gaming industry. I chose gaming because it has been a significant part of my life since an early age, and I've witnessed the incredible progress in game development throughout the years. Like many others, I began my game development journey with [Unity](https://unity.com/), which is a powerful tool for creating impressive interactive experiences with support for many platforms and has a vast community of developers who are willing to share their work and experience with others. Unity also provides an amazing platform called [Learn](https://learn.unity.com/), which allowed me to follow different pathways to learn about various aspects of game development by completing tasks and putting my skills into practice. I have completed [Unity Essentials](https://learn.unity.com/pathway/unity-essentials), [Junior Programmer](https://learn.unity.com/pathway/junior-programmer), and [Creative Core](https://learn.unity.com/pathway/creative-core) pathways, and gained a great deal of knowledge about game development with Unity.

Currently, I am working on some small projects of my own and continuing to learn about new technologies, such as Unity [DOTS](https://unity.com/dots), so I can incorporate them into my games to make them more appealing to players. I will be sharing my progress on this website and plan to publish some of my games on platforms such as [itch.io](https://itch.io/).
